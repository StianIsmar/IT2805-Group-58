P4: Project Testing

// Group 58: Ophelia Amalie Prillard, Kari Anne Dalheim, Stian Ismar, Morten Olsen Osvik
// 10. October 2019

Administrative Details

Antikvariatet is a cozy bar/pub/café/venue located at Bakklandet in Trondheim. It offers a great assortment of beers as well as outside serving. The inside of Antikvariatet consists of two separated areas. One offers a calm, laid-back atmosphere while the other hosts various kinds of events. The events ranges from small concerts and standup comedy to cultural performances and social debates.

Testing Document

Instead of using a Google Form with questions, we decided to ask the test person a list of questions face to face. We decided to do this mainly because it takes less time and we were more likely to get better responses, because we were able to ask follow-up questions etc.

This idea was inspired by an article we found on usertesting.com.

It states the following: “It’s also a good idea to run moderated tests if you can. While you can conduct unmoderated tests with prototypes, chances are your participants will have questions and some tasks will require more explanation and guidance, so having a moderator present will help you get the best feedback.”

The Questions:

  1. What do you think about the design?
  2. How was navigating the web page?
  3. What are you thoughts surrounding the content?
  4. How was the usability? Was everything clear?
  5. What were you missing from the website?


More importantly, as the user was testing the webpage, we were able to look over their shoulder to see how they maneuvered the page. This makes it easy to spot frustration and confusing design layout, just by looking at where the test person clicks, as well as their facial expressions.

Tester Audience

We wanted to focus on having quality over quantity for our testing, i. e. interviewing fewer participants but doing it more thoroughly.

Who We Interviewed:

Everyone in our group interviewed 2 people each. Stian interviewed two people living at Bakklandet at the age of 22 and 30. The younger test person is studying computer science at the university. Morten interviewd one student studying design and one 60 year old person living in Oslo. Ophelia interviewd one 23 year old student studding dance and a 28 year old friend living at Singsaker. Kari Anne interviewd her mother at 56 and her room-mate studying architecture.

Feedback Summary

The feedback from our test subjects is summarized below.


Positive:

  1. Professional looking website
  2. Nice atmosphere
  3. Clean website
  4. Likes the map feature and that it is possible to move around and zoom.
  5. The older generation liked the possibility to scroll down to see the content. Without having to navigate the mouse to the button
  6. Liked the event layout
  7. Liked the facebook, instagram and twitter logos in footer
  8. Liked the gray background box surrounding menu, events and contact info

Negative:

  1. Font size a bit too big in the navbar
  2. Too much white space between the main content of the website and the footer
  3. A little too much white space between the main content of the website and the footer
  4. It was not obvious that the event links were clickable, especially on mobile
  5. Early homepage picture looked a bit boring
  6. Restaurant picture looked weird on mobile
  7. Events were a little crowded. Should be more space
  8. Disliked the font. Looked old/outdated
  9. The arrow button was too small
  10. The picture for homepage could show even more of Antikvariatet to give an even better impression of what the place looks like. Would also like more pictures showing what it looks like inside
  11. The search bar looked out of place
  12. Did not like the gray background box surrounding menu, events and contact info
  13. Thought the arrow-button was too slow to load the content

Our Response

All feedback was taken into consideration and the following changes were made:

  1. Navbar font size was made smaller
  2. Put the footer closer to the main content by removing white space
  3. A button to get the page in norwegian was considered as something that would be nice to have but not necessary. It was not developed but would be useful if we were to launch it
  4. We added a “More info” button underneathn each event insted of having users clicking the text
  5. The header pictures were replaced
  6. Proper media queries were implemented and a differently cropped restaurant picture loads when the page is smaller than 880px
  7. We added more space to the event list when the “More info” buttons were introduced
  8. The font-family was changed
  9. The arrow button’s size was increased
  10. Antikvariatet did not have any better pictures than those we had. We considered taking the photos ourselves but did not have time to do it
  11. The search bar was moved closer to the event list
  12. Some liked the gray background and others did not. We liked it and decided to keep it
  13. The fade effect animation was reduced to 3 seconds

Self-reflection

We have learned more about HTML, CSS and JS, and how it is working with these technologies in a team. The last part is very relevant for future work. We also used Git to deal with version control and merging our code which was great once we got to know it.
