Testing Document

Instead of using a Google Form with questions, we decided to ask the test person a list of questions face to face. We decided to do this mainly because it takes less time and we were more likely to get better responses, because we were able to ask follow-up questions etc.

This idea was inspired by an article we found on usertesting.com.

It states the following: “It’s also a good idea to run moderated tests if you can. While you can conduct unmoderated tests with prototypes, chances are your participants will have questions and some tasks will require more explanation and guidance, so having a moderator present will help you get the best feedback.”

The Questions:

  1. What do you think about the design?
  2. How was navigating the web page?
  3. What are you thoughts surrounding the content?
  4. How was the usability? Was everything clear?
  5. What were you missing from the website?


More importantly, as the user was testing the webpage, we were able to look over their shoulder to see how they maneuvered the page. This makes it easy to spot frustration and confusing design layout, just by looking at the where the test person clicks, as well as their facial expressions.

Tester Audience

We wanted to focus on having quality over quantity for our testing, i. e. interviewing fewer participants but doing it more thoroughly.

Who We Interviewed:

Everyone in our group interviewed 2 people each. Stian interviewed two people living at Bakklandet at the age of 22 and 30. The younger test person is studying computer science at the university. Morten interviewd one student studying design and one 60 year old person living in Oslo. Ophelia interviewd one 23 years old student studding dance and a 28 years old friend living at Singsaker. Kari Anne interviewd her mother at 56 and her room-mate studying architecture.

Feedback Summary

The feedback from our test subjects is summarized below.


Positive:

  • Professional looking website
  • Nice atmosphere
  • Clean website
  • Likes the map feature and that it is possible to move around and zoom.
  • The older generation liked the possibility to scroll down to see the content. Without having to navigate the mouse to the button
  • Liked the event layout
  • Liked the facebook, instagram and twitter logos in footer
  • Liked the gray background box surrounding menu, events and contact info

Negative:

  1. Font size a bit too big in the navbar
  2. Too much white space between the main content of the website and the footer
  3. It was not obvious that the event links were clickable, especially on mobile.
